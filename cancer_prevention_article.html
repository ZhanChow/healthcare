Part One

The Six Risk Factors That Matter Most

According to WHO, over 50% of cancers in East Asian men may be preventable — the highest rate globally. Everyday habits drive much of that risk. Here are the six most significant modifiable factors in our region:

🚬
SE Asia: highest tobacco use globally
Tobacco
Tobacco use in SE Asia (26.5%) exceeds the global average. In Singapore, lung cancer is a top-3 killer. Cigars, vaping, and secondhand smoke all carry real risk.
🍔
Colorectal: #2 cancer in SG (both sexes)
Diet & Body Weight
Colorectal cancer — closely tied to diet — is Singapore's 2nd most common cancer. Rising rates reflect Asia's shift toward processed, Western-style eating.
🍷
Liver cancer: among highest rates in Asia
Alcohol
A known Group 1 carcinogen linked to 7 cancer types. Liver cancer rates in Asia are among the world's highest — alcohol is a significant contributor alongside Hepatitis B.
☀️
High UV year-round in Singapore
UV Radiation
Singapore's equatorial location means high UV exposure 365 days a year. Skin cancer rates are rising. Sunscreen (SPF 30+) and shade make a measurable difference.
🛋️
150 min/week is the target
Physical Inactivity
Sedentary lifestyles are a growing concern across urban Asia. Regular movement reduces inflammation and regulates cancer-promoting hormones.
🦠
HBV endemic across much of Asia
Infections (HPV, HBV)
Hepatitis B — endemic across parts of Asia — drives high liver cancer rates regionally. HPV causes cervical and throat cancers. Vaccines protect against both.

Tobacco: Still the Biggest Risk

Tobacco causes roughly 30% of all cancer deaths globally. In Singapore, lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer death in men and 3rd most common in women. Cigars, vaping, and secondhand smoke all carry real risk.

"Quitting is the single most impactful lifestyle change a person can make for their cancer risk — at any age."

Cancer risk starts falling almost immediately after quitting. After 10 years, lung cancer risk drops to roughly half. Medication and behavioural support significantly improve success rates. There is no shame in needing help.

Diet & Body Weight

Colorectal cancer is Singapore's 2nd most common cancer for both men and women, strongly tied to diet. As Asian societies shift toward processed food, rates are rising — even in people under 50. Small shifts compound into real protection:

Evidence-backed dietary shifts for our region:

🥦 More plants — fruits, vegetables, legumes, and wholegrains consistently lower risk

🥩 Less processed meat — WHO classifies it as a Group 1 carcinogen; limit char siu, luncheon meat, sausages

🫒 Eat like your grandparents — fish, tofu, vegetables, brown rice; minimise processed and packaged food

Alcohol

Alcohol is a Group 1 carcinogen linked to breast, colon, liver, and oesophageal cancers. Asia's liver cancer rates are among the world's highest — alcohol and Hepatitis B are key drivers. Crucially, even moderate, regular drinking raises risk. There is no safe level.

If cutting back feels difficult: Start with a few alcohol-free days per week. If you feel dependent, speaking with a GP is a brave and important step.

Screening: Catch It Early

Screening catches cancer before symptoms appear — when treatment is most effective. In Singapore, the Healthier SG Screening programme (formerly Screen for Life) subsidises key cancer screenings for eligible citizens and PRs.
